Risk & Money Management (Ralph Vince)
[VINCE]
📖 WHAT IS THIS SECTION?
Ralph Vince was a mathematician who discovered that HOW MUCH you bet matters more than WHAT you bet on. Even a winning strategy loses money if you bet too much per trade.
🏠 Real-World Analogy: Imagine you're a professional poker player with a winning strategy. Vince's math answers: "You have ₹1,00,000. You have an edge. How much should you bet on each hand so you grow the fastest WITHOUT going bankrupt?" Bet too much → one bad hand wipes you out. Bet too little → you grow too slowly. Vince found the mathematically optimal bet size.
Composite Risk Score (0–100)
Higher = Safer. Combines drawdown, volatility, position sizing & profitability into one number.
Optimal f
Optimal f = the mathematically ideal fraction of your capital to risk on each trade to maximize long-term growth. Example: if Optimal f = 25%, risk ₹25,000 per trade on a ₹1,00,000 account. We use 50% of this (conservative) so you grow steadily without stomach-churning drawdowns.
Kelly f
Kelly Criterion = another formula (from gambling theory) for optimal bet size, based on your win rate and win/loss ratio. If Kelly f = 30%, it means you win often enough and big enough that risking 30% is mathematically optimal. Compare with Optimal f — if both agree, the sizing is very reliable.
Geometric Mean
Geometric Mean HPR = the average growth factor per trade. Above 1.0 = you're making money on average. Below 1.0 = losing. Example: 1.0005 means each trade grows your account by 0.05% on average. Even tiny numbers above 1.0 compound into large gains over hundreds of trades.
TWR
Terminal Wealth Relative = if you had traded at optimal f for every single trade in this stock's history, your money would have multiplied by this number. TWR of 2.5 means ₹1,00,000 becomes ₹2,50,000. Warning: This assumes perfect execution and is theoretical.
Max Drawdown
Max Drawdown = the worst peak-to-valley loss this stock has experienced. If it says -25%, that means at some point the stock dropped 25% from its high. This is the WORST-CASE pain level you must be prepared for. If you can't stomach a 25% drop, reduce your position size.
Volatility
Volatility = how wildly the stock price swings day to day (annualized). Low volatility (under 20%) = calm, predictable stock. High volatility (over 40%) = wild swings, exciting but dangerous. Think of it as the "bumpiness" of the ride.
Biggest Loss
Biggest Single-Day Loss = the largest amount this stock dropped in ONE day. This is critically important because Vince's optimal f is calculated using this number. It represents the worst single blow your capital could take.
Time to 2x
Time to Double = how many trading days it would take to double your initial investment at the current geometric growth rate. Fewer days = faster-growing stock. If it says ~500 trades, and there are ~250 trading days per year, that's about 2 years to double.
Risk Health Radar
5 axes show your risk profile. Bigger area = healthier. If one axis is very short, that's a weak spot.

Position Sizing (50% of Optimal f — Conservative)
🎯 What is this? This tells you if you're holding the right NUMBER of shares. We use 50% of Optimal f (half the max) for safety — like driving at half the speed limit on a wet road. OPTIMAL = just right. OVERSIZED = you're holding too much, at risk of big losses. UNDERSIZED = you could hold more safely.
