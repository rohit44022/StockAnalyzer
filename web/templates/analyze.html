7 Indicator Readings — 3 Groups
GroupIndicatorValueDetailsSignal

Group A — Price & Trend

Bollinger Bands — Two lines (upper & lower) drawn 2 standard deviations from a 20-day moving average. When price breaks above the upper band, it's a breakout signal.

Parabolic SAR — Dots that follow price. Green dots below = uptrend (hold). Red dots above = downtrend (exit). Acts as a trailing stop-loss.

Volume + SMA-50 — Volume is the number of shares traded. The 50-day average (yellow line) is the "normal" level. Volume above it = conviction.

Group B — Volatility

BandWidth (BBW) — Measures how wide the Bollinger Bands are. Low BBW (below 0.08 or near 6-month minimum) = squeeze is ON. Think of it as a coiled spring.

%b (Percent B) — Shows where price sits within the bands. 0 = at lower band, 0.5 = middle, 1.0 = at upper band. Above 0.80 = bullish zone.

Group C — Money Flow

CMF (Chaikin Money Flow) — Tracks whether money is flowing INTO the stock (+) or OUT (−). Above +0.10 = strong accumulation by big players. Below −0.10 = big players are selling.

MFI (Money Flow Index) — Like RSI but includes volume. Above 50 = sufficient buying fuel. Above 80 = maximum fuel. Below 20 = oversold.

How to Use This Strategy — Beginner's Guide

Wait for ALL 5 conditions to turn green (✅). This means the spring has been coiled (squeeze), the price has broken out above the upper band, volume confirms the move is real, and money flow (CMF & MFI) says big players are buying. Buy at the next market open after all 5 conditions are met. Set your stop-loss at the Parabolic SAR value shown.

Key indicators to watch:
  • BBW — below 0.08 or near its 6-month minimum = squeeze is active
  • %B — must be above 1.0 (price above upper band) for breakout confirmation
  • Volume — must be above the yellow SMA-50 line
  • CMF — must be positive (above zero line on the chart)
  • MFI — must be above 50 and rising

If you're already in a trade: keep holding as long as SAR dots are green (below candles) — this means the uptrend is intact. Trail your stop-loss to the SAR value every day. The SAR automatically tightens as the trend progresses, locking in profits.

Additional hold signals:
  • %B stays above 0.50 — price is in the upper half of bands (bullish)
  • MFI stays above 50 — buying fuel is still present
  • CMF remains positive — big players still buying
  • Method IV "Walking the Bands" signal is active — strong trend continuation

Exit on any of these three signals:
1. SAR Flip — dots flip from green (below) to red (above). The trend has reversed.
2. Lower Band Tag — price touches or closes below the lower Bollinger Band. Momentum is gone.
3. Double Negative — both CMF goes below 0 AND MFI drops below 50. Money is flowing out.
Any ONE of these is enough to exit. Don't wait for all three.

Reading the sub-charts for exit:
  • %B dropping below 0.20 on the %B chart = bearish zone, price near lower band
  • MFI dropping below 20 on the MFI chart = buying fuel exhausted
  • CMF crossing below zero on the CMF chart = money flowing OUT
  • Method III M-Top pattern detected = classic reversal signal to sell

A head fake is when price breaks above the upper band but volume, CMF, and MFI do NOT confirm the move. It's a false breakout — the REAL move will come in the opposite direction within 2-3 days. Golden Rule: Never buy a breakout without volume above the yellow line (50-day SMA).

How to spot it on the charts:
  • %B spikes above 1.0 but quickly reverses — false breakout
  • Volume bars are below the yellow SMA line — no conviction
  • CMF is negative — big players are actually selling, not buying
  • MFI is below 50 — not enough fuel to sustain the move

Phase 1 — COMPRESSION: Bands are tight (BBW at squeeze trigger). The spring is being coiled. Low volatility. Watch CMF for direction clues.
Phase 2 — DIRECTION CLUES: Still in squeeze, but big players are starting to position. CMF and MFI are giving hints about which way the breakout will go.
Phase 3 — EXPLOSION: Bands expand rapidly. The spring has been released! This is where the trade happens — enter on the breakout day if all 5 conditions are met.

On the BBW chart: Watch the purple line. When it's at its lowest point (below 0.08 dashed red line) = Phase 1. When it starts rising sharply = Phase 3 explosion.

John Bollinger's Method II uses %B and MFI together to confirm trend direction. It's simpler than Method I (no squeeze needed).

BUY when: %B is above 0.80 AND MFI is above 80 — both indicators say "strongly bullish."
SELL when: %B is below 0.20 AND MFI is below 20 — both say "strongly bearish."
On the charts: Look at the %B panel (above 0.80 line) and MFI panel (above 80 green line) simultaneously. Both must be in the extreme zone.

Book reference: Bollinger on Bollinger Bands, Chapter 16 — "Method II: Trend Following."

Method III looks for classic reversal patterns — W-Bottoms (double bottom = buy) and M-Tops (double top = sell).

W-Bottom (BUY): Price makes a low near/below the lower band, bounces, pulls back again but this time %B is HIGHER than the first low (divergence). This creates a "W" shape — a strong reversal signal.
M-Top (SELL): Price makes a high near/above the upper band, pulls back, rallies again but %B is LOWER (divergence). This creates an "M" shape — sell signal.

On the charts: Watch the %B panel for divergence — price making a new low/high but %B not confirming. That divergence is the key signal.

Book reference: Bollinger on Bollinger Bands, Chapter 17 — "Method III: Reversals."

Method IV identifies strong trends where price "walks" along the upper or lower Bollinger Band for extended periods.

Upper Band Walk (BULLISH): Price repeatedly touches/exceeds the upper band. %B stays above 0.80 for multiple days. Volume confirms. This is a STRONG uptrend — hold or add to position.
Lower Band Walk (BEARISH): Price repeatedly touches the lower band. %B stays below 0.20. EXIT immediately — the stock is in a sustained downtrend.

On the charts: Look at the %B panel — if %B stays consistently above 0.80 (hugging the top) or below 0.20 (hugging the bottom), the stock is "walking the band."

Book reference: Bollinger on Bollinger Bands, Chapter 18 — "Method IV: Walking the Bands."

The Conviction tab in Fundamentals gives you a BUY / HOLD / AVOID verdict based on 4 pillars:

Valuation (25%) — Is the stock cheap or expensive? P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, PEG, Graham Number.
Profitability (30%) — Is the company making money? ROE, margins, EBITDA.
Growth (25%) — Is it growing? Revenue growth, earnings growth, EPS trend.
Stability (20%) — Is it financially safe? Debt/Equity, current ratio, Altman Z-Score.

Best approach: Use fundamentals for WHAT to buy (strong companies) and Bollinger Bands for WHEN to buy (timing the entry). A stock with BUY verdict + 5 conditions met = high conviction trade.
