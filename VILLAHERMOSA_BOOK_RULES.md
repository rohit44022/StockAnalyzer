## SECTION 1 — COMPOSITE MAN RULES [VILLAHERMOSA]

> The Composite Man is the aggregation of all large professional operators
> (institutions, funds, market makers) whose accumulated activity drives
> price. Villahermosa's core premise: align your trading with the Composite Man.

### Rule CM1: Think Like the Composite Man
- The Composite Man accumulates stock at lows (buying from weak hands).
- The Composite Man distributes stock at highs (selling to greedy latecomers).
- The Composite Man engineers shakeouts (Springs/UTAD) to capture liquidity.
- **Actionable:** Before every trade ask: "What is the Composite Man likely doing?"

### Rule CM2: Counterparty Requirement
- Large operators NEED counterparties: to buy, they need sellers; to sell, they need buyers.
- Springs create sellers (stop-loss triggers + panic) → Composite Man buys from them.
- UTADs create buyers (breakout traders + FOMO) → Composite Man sells to them.
- **Actionable:** When you see a Spring/UTAD, the Composite Man is taking the OTHER side.

### Rule CM3: Path of Least Resistance
- Before initiating the trend, the Composite Man tests the path of least resistance.
- If a test shows high participation by the opposing side → more absorption needed.
- If a test shows low participation → the path is clear, trend movement begins.
- **Actionable:** After a Spring, if the Test shows LOW volume → path is clear = ENTER.
  If the Test shows HIGH volume → more testing needed = WAIT.

### Rule CM4: Stock Control Transfer
- Accumulation = transfer from weak hands (fearful, reactive) to strong hands (professional, patient).
- Distribution = transfer from strong hands to weak hands (greedy, late).
- **Actionable:** Decreasing volume during a range = absorption in progress = transfer happening.

## SECTION 2 — WYCKOFF 3 LAWS RULES [VILLAHERMOSA]

### LAW 1: SUPPLY AND DEMAND

### Rule SD1: Price Moves by Imbalance
- Price rises when aggressive demand exceeds available supply.
- Price falls when aggressive supply exceeds available demand.
- Lateral movement = equilibrium between supply and demand.
- **Actionable:** Identify which side is AGGRESSIVE (initiative) vs PASSIVE (responsive).

### Rule SD2: Aggressive vs Passive Orders
- Aggressive orders: market orders that CROSS the spread to execute immediately.
- Passive orders: limit orders that WAIT at a price level.
- Initiative activity: aggressive orders in a direction = genuine intent.
- Lack of interest: passive orders only, no aggressive crossing = no conviction.
- **Actionable:** High volume + wide spread + close in direction = initiative (real move).
  High volume + narrow spread = passive absorption (blocked move).

### Rule SD3: Four Scenarios at Any Price Level
| Scenario | Volume | Spread | Close | Meaning |
|----------|--------|--------|-------|---------|
| Initiative buying | High | Wide | Near high | Aggressive buyers = bullish |
| Initiative selling | High | Wide | Near low | Aggressive sellers = bearish |
| Lack of demand | Low | Narrow | Indifferent | No buyers = potential weakness |
| Lack of supply | Low | Narrow | Indifferent | No sellers = potential strength |

### LAW 2: CAUSE AND EFFECT

### Rule CE1: Lateral Ranges Build Cause
- Every lateral trading range builds a CAUSE.
- The subsequent trend movement is the EFFECT.
- The DURATION of the cause is proportional to the DISTANCE of the effect.
- **Actionable:** Longer accumulation range → larger subsequent uptrend.
  Longer distribution range → larger subsequent downtrend.

### Rule CE2: Point and Figure Counting (Target Estimation)
- Count columns across the congestion zone × reversal unit = potential price target.
- Conservative count: count to nearest internal wall.
- Maximum count: count full span of the structure.
- **Actionable:** Use P&F counts as GUIDELINES, not absolutes. They provide
  approximate targets for where the Composite Man may take profits.

### LAW 3: EFFORT VS RESULT

### Rule ER1: Volume = Effort, Price = Result
- High effort + High result = HARMONY → trend continues (genuine move).
- High effort + Low result = DIVERGENCE → absorption (smart money blocking).
- Low effort + High result = EASE OF MOVEMENT → no opposition, path clear.
- Low effort + Low result = NO INTEREST → skip, wait for action.
- **Actionable:** Always compare volume (effort) to price spread (result) on EVERY bar.

### Rule ER2: Effort vs Result on Individual Candles
- Bullish candle with high volume closing near high = bullish harmony.
- Bullish candle with high volume closing near low = hidden selling (divergence).
- Bearish candle with high volume closing near low = bearish harmony.
- Bearish candle with high volume closing near high = hidden buying (divergence).

### Rule ER3: Effort vs Result on Movements (Multi-Bar)
- Impulsive movement: wide ranges, expanding volume = harmony with trend.
- Corrective movement: narrow ranges, contracting volume = healthy correction.
- If corrective movement shows wide ranges + high volume → NOT a correction,
  it's a change of character = potential reversal.

### Rule ER4: Effort vs Result on Waves (Cumulative)
- Compare cumulative volume on up-waves vs down-waves.
- If up-wave volume INCREASING while down-wave volume DECREASING = demand dominant.
- If down-wave volume INCREASING while up-wave volume DECREASING = supply dominant.
- **Actionable:** Wave-level analysis is MORE reliable than bar-level analysis.

### Rule ER5: Effort vs Result at Key Levels
- At support: high volume + price holds = demand absorbing supply = bullish.
- At resistance: high volume + price holds = supply absorbing demand = bearish.
- At support: low volume + price drops through = no demand = bearish.
- At resistance: low volume + price rises through = no supply = bullish (ease of movement).

## SECTION 3 — 7 EVENTS IDENTIFICATION RULES [VILLAHERMOSA]

### EVENT #1: PRELIMINARY STOP (PS / PSY)

### Rule E1.1: Preliminary Support (PS) — Accumulation
- First significant attempt to stop the downtrend.
- Characteristics: notable increase in volume + wider price range.
- The PS FAILS to stop the decline permanently — price will make new lows.
- **Actionable:** PS appearance = first alert that smart money is interested.
  Do NOT buy here. Wait for Selling Climax.

### Rule E1.2: Preliminary Supply (PSY) — Distribution
- First significant attempt to stop the uptrend.
- Characteristics: notable increase in volume + wider price range at highs.
- The PSY FAILS to stop the advance — price may make new highs.
- **Actionable:** PSY appearance = first alert that smart money is selling.
  Do NOT sell here. Wait for Buying Climax.

### EVENT #2: CLIMAX (SC / BC)

### Rule E2.1: Selling Climax (SC) — Identification
- Panicked selling after the PS, price accelerates downward.
- Volume: EXTREME — highest of the entire decline.
- Spread: WIDE — one of the largest down-bars.
- Close: NEAR THE HIGH of the bar (smart money buying the panic).
- Sets the LOWER boundary of the trading range.
- **Binary Test:** Volume > 3× average AND spread in top 20% AND close in upper 40% of range
  → SELLING CLIMAX confirmed.

### Rule E2.2: Buying Climax (BC) — Identification
- Euphoric buying after the PSY, price accelerates upward.
- Volume: EXTREME — highest of the entire advance.
- Spread: WIDE — one of the largest up-bars.
- Close: NEAR THE LOW of the bar (smart money selling into euphoria).
- Sets the UPPER boundary of the trading range.
- **Binary Test:** Volume > 3× average AND spread in top 20% AND close in lower 40% of range
  → BUYING CLIMAX confirmed.

### Rule E2.3: Exhaustion Variants (Selling Exhaustion / Buying Exhaustion)
- Same function as Climax but WITHOUT extreme volume.
- The trend simply "runs out of steam" on declining volume.
- Identified by progressive volume decrease on the final wave.
- **Actionable:** If a trend stops without a volume spike, this is exhaustion,
  not climax. The range that follows still serves the same purpose.

### EVENT #3: REACTION (AR)

### Rule E3.1: Automatic Rally (AR) — After Selling Climax
- After the SC, aggressive buyers push price upward.
- Represents the FIRST Change of Character (CHoCH #1) — first significant move
  against the prior downtrend.
- Sets the UPPER boundary of the trading range.
- Volume: should be notably less than the SC.
- **Actionable:** AR establishes the resistance level. Do NOT buy the AR — it
  is a reaction, not the start of the uptrend.

### Rule E3.2: Automatic Reaction (AR) — After Buying Climax
- After the BC, aggressive sellers push price downward.
- First Change of Character — first significant move against the prior uptrend.
- Sets the LOWER boundary of the trading range.
- **Actionable:** AR establishes the support level for the distributive range.

### EVENT #4: TEST (ST / Secondary Test)

### Rule E4.1: Secondary Test (ST) in Phase A
- Price returns to the SC/BC area to test supply/demand.
- CRITICAL: Volume MUST be LESS than on the Climax.
- If volume on ST < SC volume → supply is drying up = bullish confirmation.
- If volume on ST ≥ SC volume → supply is still present = bearish, more time needed.
- **Binary Test:** Price within 2% of SC low AND volume < 75% of SC volume
  → SUCCESSFUL TEST. Confirms Phase A completion.

### Rule E4.2: Secondary Test in Phase B (ST in B)
- Tests at BOTH extremes of the range during Phase B.
- At the upper extreme: Upthrust Action (UA) or Upthrust (UT) — fails to break resistance.
- At the lower extreme: Secondary Test as Sign of Weakness (ST as SOW).
- Function: building the cause, absorbing remaining stock.
- **Actionable:** Multiple tests in Phase B are NORMAL. Each test on declining
  volume confirms absorption is progressing.

### Rule E4.3: Generic Test (No Supply / No Demand)
- NO SUPPLY: Down bar + LOW volume + NARROW spread at support area.
  Confirms sellers have disappeared. Bullish.
- NO DEMAND: Up bar + LOW volume + NARROW spread at resistance area.
  Confirms buyers have disappeared. Bearish.
- **Actionable:** No Supply at support after a Spring = strong bullish confirmation.
  No Demand at resistance after UTAD = strong bearish confirmation.

### EVENT #5: SHAKING (Spring / UTAD)

### Rule E5.1: Spring — Identification
- Price breaks BELOW the trading range low (support/SC low) and REVERSES back inside.
- The break is BRIEF — typically 1-3 bars outside the range.
- **This is Phase C's primary event in accumulation structures.**

### Rule E5.2: Spring Types (Villahermosa Classification)
| Type | Volume | Size | Market Response | Tradeable? |
|------|--------|------|-----------------|------------|
| Spring #1 (Terminal Shakeout) | VERY HIGH | Large penetration | Wide-spread reversal bar | Risky — HIGH vol needs retest |
| Spring #2 | MODERATE | Medium penetration | Moderate response | Wait for confirmation test |
| Spring #3 | LOW | Small penetration | Quiet recovery | BEST — immediately tradeable |

- **Actionable Rule:** If volume on Spring < volume on SC → this is Spring #3 (BEST).
  If volume on Spring ≈ SC → this is Spring #1/2 → WAIT for secondary test.
  If volume on Spring > SC → this is NOT a Spring, genuine breakdown.

### Rule E5.3: Spring Triple Function
The Spring simultaneously:
1. STOPS out weak holders below support (captures their stop-losses).
2. INDUCES new short sellers to enter (creates counterparty for Composite Man's buys).
3. PROFITS the Composite Man who buys at the best possible price.
- **Actionable:** After a Spring, there should be trapped shorts who will need
  to cover (adding fuel to the rally). The more trapped shorts = bigger rally.

### Rule E5.4: UTAD (Upthrust After Distribution) — Identification
- Price breaks ABOVE the trading range high (resistance/BC high) and REVERSES back inside.
- Mirror of Spring for distributive structures.
- Volume behavior: same classification as Spring types.
- **Actionable:** If volume on UTAD < volume on BC → UTAD #3 (BEST short signal).
  If volume on UTAD ≈ BC → UTAD #1/2 → WAIT for secondary test.

### Rule E5.5: Shakeout vs Spring Distinction [VILLAHERMOSA]
| Feature | Spring | Terminal Shakeout |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Penetration | Small (1-3%) | Large (>3%) |
| Volume | Low to moderate | Very high (climactic) |
| Duration | 1-3 bars | Can be multi-bar structure |
| Recovery | Quick reversal | May need extensive retesting |
| Tradability | Direct entry possible | Must wait for test |

### Rule E5.6: Spring That Appears as Minor Structure
- Sometimes the Spring manifests as a small structure BELOW the range (1-3 bars or more).
- This "minor structure" may look like a mini-accumulation.
- **Actionable:** If price builds a small structure below support with declining volume
  and then reverses → treat as Spring. The trigger is the break back INTO the range.

### EVENT #6: BREAKOUT (SOS / SOW)

### Rule E6.1: Sign of Strength (SOS) / Jump Across the Creek (JAC)
- Impulsive upward movement that breaks above the Creek (resistance/AR high).
- Characteristics: wide range bars + increasing volume + close near high.
- This is Change of Character #2 (CHoCH #2) — confirms the bullish imbalance.
- **Binary Test:** Price closes ABOVE the Creek AND volume > 1.3× average AND
  spread in top 40% → SOS CONFIRMED.
- **CRITICAL:** The breakout itself is NOT an entry opportunity.
  Wait for the Confirmation (Event #7).

### Rule E6.2: SOS Bar (Sign of Strength Bar) — Entry Trigger
- A specific wide-range bullish bar with:
  - Range wider than the previous 5 bars' average.
  - Volume above average.
  - Close in the upper half of its range.
  - Close above a previous resistance level.
- **Actionable:** SOS Bar appearance during Phase D = entry trigger for long.

### Rule E6.3: Sign of Weakness (SOW) / Major SOW
- Impulsive downward movement that breaks below the Ice (support/AR low).
- Characteristics: wide range bars + increasing volume + close near low.
- **Binary Test:** Price closes BELOW the Ice AND volume > 1.3× average AND
  spread in top 40% → SOW CONFIRMED.

### Rule E6.4: SOW Bar (Sign of Weakness Bar) — Entry Trigger
- A specific wide-range bearish bar (mirror of SOS Bar).
- **Actionable:** SOW Bar appearance during Phase D (distribution) = entry trigger for short.

### EVENT #7: CONFIRMATION (LPS / LPSY / BUEC)

### Rule E7.1: Last Point of Support (LPS) — Accumulation Confirmation
- After the SOS/JAC breakout, price pulls back to test the broken Creek.
- CRITICAL characteristics:
  - Narrow range candles (interlaced, overlapping).
  - DECREASING volume (lack of selling interest).
  - Price does NOT re-enter the range.
- If price holds above the Creek on low volume → Accumulation CONFIRMED.
- **Binary Test:** Pullback reaches Creek area AND volume < 75% of breakout volume
  AND price stays above Creek → LPS CONFIRMED = primary long entry.

### Rule E7.2: Back Up to the Edge of the Creek (BUEC)
- Synonym for LPS — the pullback movement that tests the Creek after breakout.
- Think of it as "the boy scout backing up to take a running jump across the creek."
- **Actionable:** BUEC = LPS = same event. Use as primary entry point.

### Rule E7.3: Last Point of Supply (LPSY) — Distribution Confirmation
- After the SOW breakout below Ice, price rallies back to test the broken Ice.
- Characteristics: narrow range, low volume rally.
- If price fails to re-enter the range → Distribution CONFIRMED.
- **Binary Test:** Rally reaches Ice area AND volume < 75% of breakdown volume
  AND price stays below Ice → LPSY CONFIRMED = primary short entry.

### Rule E7.4: Fall Through the Ice (FTI)
- Synonym for LPSY confirmation — price falls through the broken Ice support.
- **Actionable:** FTI = LPSY = same event. Use as primary short entry point.

### Rule E7.5: Warning — High Volume on Confirmation Test
- If the LPS/LPSY pullback shows RELATIVELY HIGH volume → CAUTION.
- High volume on the test means there IS still interest in the opposing direction.
- Wait for successive tests until volume diminishes.
- If the pullback has wide ranges + high volume → likely NOT a confirmation,
  and the breakout may become a Shaking event (failed break → re-entry into range).

## SECTION 4 — 5 PHASES IDENTIFICATION RULES [VILLAHERMOSA]

### PHASE A: STOPPING THE PREVIOUS TREND

### Rule PA1: Phase A Components
Phase A consists of the first four events in order:
1. Preliminary Stop (PS/PSY)
2. Climax (SC/BC)
3. Reaction (AR)
4. Secondary Test (ST)

### Rule PA2: Phase A Completion Criteria
- Phase A is COMPLETE when the Secondary Test holds above/below the Climax level
  with declining volume.
- **Binary Test:** ST volume < SC/BC volume AND price holds within range
  → Phase A COMPLETE. Phase B begins.

### Rule PA3: Phase A Volume Behavior
- PS: First significant volume increase after declining trend volume.
- SC/BC: HIGHEST volume of the entire range — peak of panic/euphoria.
- AR: Volume should be LESS than Climax. If equal or higher → flag as anomalous.
- ST: Volume MUST be less than Climax. If not → Phase A is NOT complete.

### PHASE B: BUILDING THE CAUSE

### Rule PB1: Phase B Function
- The Composite Man absorbs the majority of remaining stock during Phase B.
- Phase B consists of successive tests at BOTH extremes of the range.
- Tests appear as: UA (Upthrust Action), UT (Upthrust), ST as SOW (minor weakness).

### Rule PB2: Phase B Duration
- Phase B SHOULD be the LONGEST phase — proportionally greater than A and C.
- If Phase B is SHORTER than Phase A or C → this denotes URGENCY by the Composite Man.
  Urgency = stronger subsequent trend movement.
- **Actionable:** Very short Phase B → expect explosive breakout.
  Very long Phase B → expect measured, sustained trend.

### Rule PB3: Phase B Volume Behavior
- Overall volume should DECREASE progressively throughout Phase B.
- This progressive decrease = stock absorption in progress.
- Individual volume spikes at range extremes are normal (tests).
- **Binary Test:** Average volume in last third of Phase B < average volume in first third
  → Absorption progressing normally.

### PHASE C: THE TEST

### Rule PC1: Phase C Events
- Phase C begins with the Shaking event and ends after its test:
  - ACCUMULATION: Spring / Shakeout → Test of Spring
  - DISTRIBUTION: UTAD → Test of UTAD

### Rule PC2: Phase C Without Sweep
- The Shaking event does NOT necessarily sweep the extremes of the structure.
- A Spring can occur WITHOUT reaching the SC low (minor Spring).
- A UTAD can occur WITHOUT reaching the BC high (minor UTAD).
- Label these as: Spring/UTAD minor, or Spring/UTAD in function of LPS/LPSY.
- **Actionable:** If the range shows extreme strength/weakness, Phase C may be
  a minor shake that doesn't reach the extremes. Still valid.

### Rule PC3: Phase C Failure — Campaign Abandoned
- If the test after the Shake shows HIGH participation (high volume):
  - The opposing side still has too many traders.
  - Stock absorption is NOT complete.
  - TWO outcomes: (a) Phase B extends with more testing, or (b) the Composite Man
    abandons the campaign and price continues in the original trend direction.
- **Actionable:** High volume on Spring test = DO NOT ENTER. Wait for more testing
  or watch for price breaking the Spring low (campaign failure).

### Rule PC4: Phase C Volume Behavior
- Spring/UTAD: LOWER volume than the Climax = BEST signal (Spring #3).
- Test of Spring/UTAD: Even LOWER volume = confirming no opposition.
- **Binary Test:** Spring vol < SC vol AND Test vol < Spring vol → Phase C CONFIRMED.

### PHASE D: TREND WITHIN THE RANGE

### Rule PD1: Phase D Events
- ACCUMULATION: SOS/JAC (breakout) → LPS/BUEC (confirmation).
- DISTRIBUTION: SOW/Major SOW (breakdown) → LPSY/FTI (confirmation).

### Rule PD2: Phase D Expected Behavior
- After Phase C test succeeds, expect clear trend movement toward the Creek/Ice.
- Wide candles + increased volume → impulsive movement confirming direction.
- **Binary Test:** After Spring test, next 3-5 bars show expanding volume +
  higher closes → Phase D developing normally.

### Rule PD3: Phase D Failure — False Break
- If breakout attempt reaches the Creek/Ice and faces too much opposition
  (high volume blocking), TWO scenarios:
  1. Price falls back to LPS/LPSY within range → takes a "running start" → tries again.
  2. The break fails entirely → becomes a NEW Shaking event in the opposite direction.
- **Actionable:** If breakout bar has extreme volume + reversal close = POTENTIAL
  false break. Wait for next bar confirmation.

### Rule PD4: Phase D Volume Behavior
- Breakout movement: volume INCREASES + wide ranges = genuine imbalance.
- Confirmation pullback (LPS/BUEC): volume DECREASES + narrow ranges = no opposition.
- **These are the optimal volume signatures for Phase D.**

### PHASE E: TREND OUT OF THE RANGE

### Rule PE1: Phase E Start
- Begins after the confirmation event (LPS/LPSY) succeeds.
- One side has ABSOLUTE control of the market.
- Only trade in the direction of the confirmed breakout.

### Rule PE2: Phase E Consists Of
- Succession of impulsive + corrective movements:
  - ACCUMULATION breakout: SOS impulses + LPS corrections.
  - DISTRIBUTION breakdown: SOW impulses + LPSY corrections.

### Rule PE3: Phase E Trading
- LPS pullbacks = opportunities to add longs (in uptrend Phase E).
- LPSY rallies = opportunities to add shorts (in downtrend Phase E).
- Phase E ends when the first events of a NEW Phase A appear
  (PS + Climax of the new cycle).

### Rule PE4: Phase E Volume Behavior
- Impulsive moves: volume EXPANDS (harmony).
- Corrective moves: volume CONTRACTS (no opposition).
- If corrective move shows EXPANDING volume = trend is ending.
  Look for Phase A events of a new structure.

## SECTION 5 — ACCUMULATION SCHEMATICS 1 AND 2 [VILLAHERMOSA]

### ACCUMULATION SCHEMATIC #1 (With Spring)

### Rule AS1.1: Schematic #1 Sequence
Phase A → PS → SC → AR → ST
Phase B → Multiple tests (UA, ST as SOW) → Volume decreasing
Phase C → **Spring** (breaks below SC low) → Test of Spring
Phase D → SOS/JAC (breaks above AR high/Creek) → LPS/BUEC
Phase E → Uptrend (SOS + LPS succession)

### Rule AS1.2: Schematic #1 Key Features
- The Spring BREAKS BELOW the SC low (sweeps the extreme).
- Phase C is clearly identifiable because of the penetration below range.
- Volume on Spring < volume on SC.
- **This is the TEXTBOOK accumulation** — easiest to identify.

### ACCUMULATION SCHEMATIC #2 (Without Spring)

### Rule AS2.1: Schematic #2 Sequence
Phase A → PS → SC → AR → ST
Phase B → Multiple tests → Volume decreasing
Phase C → **LPS** (higher low, does NOT break below SC) → mild test
Phase D → SOS/JAC → LPS/BUEC
Phase E → Uptrend

### Rule AS2.2: Schematic #2 Key Features
- Phase C shows a higher low (Last Point of Support), NOT a Spring.
- This indicates greater BACKGROUND STRENGTH — buyers so strong they don't
  let price reach the SC lows.
- More difficult to identify in real time (no dramatic Spring event).
- **Actionable:** If after Phase B the price builds a higher low with
  declining volume → likely Schematic #2.

### Rule AS2.3: How to Distinguish Before Completion
| Signal | Schematic #1 | Schematic #2 |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Phase C behavior | Breaks below SC low | Higher low within range |
| Background strength | Normal | Strong (buyers dominant) |
| Difficulty | Easier to identify | Harder to identify |
| Volume on Phase C | Low at Spring vs SC | Low, no dramatic event |
| Trend context | Works in any context | More reliable in uptrends |

## SECTION 6 — DISTRIBUTION SCHEMATICS 1 AND 2 [VILLAHERMOSA]

### DISTRIBUTION SCHEMATIC #1 (With UTAD)

### Rule DS1.1: Schematic #1 Sequence
Phase A → PSY → BC → AR → ST
Phase B → Multiple tests (minor SOW, UA) → High volume throughout
Phase C → **UTAD** (breaks above BC high) → Test of UTAD
Phase D → SOW/Major SOW (breaks below AR low/Ice) → LPSY/FTI
Phase E → Downtrend (SOW + LPSY succession)

### Rule DS1.2: Schematic #1 Key Features
- UTAD BREAKS ABOVE the BC high (sweeps the extreme).
- Volume during distribution generally remains HIGH throughout the range
  (unlike accumulation where it decreases).
- **This high volume signals urgency — large operators unloading positions.**

### DISTRIBUTION SCHEMATIC #2 (Without UTAD)

### Rule DS2.1: Schematic #2 Sequence
Phase A → PSY → BC → AR → ST
Phase B → Multiple tests → High volume
Phase C → **LPSY** (lower high, does NOT break above BC) → mild test
Phase D → SOW → LPSY/FTI
Phase E → Downtrend

### Rule DS2.2: Schematic #2 Key Features
- Phase C shows a lower high, NOT a UTAD.
- This indicates greater BACKGROUND WEAKNESS — sellers so aggressive
  they don't even let price reach the BC highs.
- **Actionable:** If after Phase B the price builds a lower high with
  persistent volume → likely Schematic #2.

### Rule DS2.3: Key Difference — Distribution Volume
- Distribution ranges generally have HIGHER and more VOLATILE volume throughout
  compared to accumulation (where volume progressively decreases).
- This is because distribution requires urgency — the Composite Man must sell
  before the decline begins.

## SECTION 7 — 3 TRADING ZONES WITH EXACT ENTRIES [VILLAHERMOSA]

### ZONE 1: PHASE C — The Shake (Highest R:R, Lowest Reliability)

### Rule Z1.1: Entry at the Shake
- Location: At the Spring low (accumulation) or UTAD high (distribution).
- BEST R:R because you enter at the extreme of the structure.
- RISK: Development of the range has had shorter duration → less certainty.
- **Entry Condition:** Spring #3 (LOW volume) detected → enter immediately.
  Spring #1/#2 (high/moderate volume) → WAIT for test.

### Rule Z1.2: Entry at the Shake Test
- After the Shake, wait for price to return to the area with:
  - Narrowing ranges (decreasing bar size).
  - Decreasing volume (less than Shake volume).
  - Price does NOT make a new extreme (holds above Spring low / below UTAD high).
- **Binary Test:** Test price > Spring low AND test volume < Spring volume
  → ENTER LONG. Stop below Spring low.

### Rule Z1.3: Zone 1 Stop and Target
- **LONG (Spring):** Stop below the Spring low. Target = Creek (AR high).
- **SHORT (UTAD):** Stop above the UTAD high. Target = Ice (AR low).
- R:R typically 3:1 or better.

### ZONE 2: PHASE D — Trend Within Range (Balanced R:R and Reliability)

### Rule Z2.1: Entry with Significant Bar (SOS/SOW Bar)
- During the trend movement from Phase C to Phase D breakout.
- Wait for SOS Bar (bullish) or SOW Bar (bearish) to appear.
- SOS Bar: range wider than 5-bar average + volume above average + close in upper half +
  close above previous resistance.
- **Entry:** At close of SOS/SOW bar, or stop order at break of its high/low.

### Rule Z2.2: Entry with Minor Structure
- Drop to a lower timeframe within the Phase D movement.
- Look for a minor re-accumulation (after Spring) or redistribution (after UTAD) structure.
- The minor structure provides a tighter entry with its own Spring/UTAD.
- **Actionable:** Minor re-accumulation = LPS of the major structure.

### Rule Z2.3: Entry at Confirmation (LPS/BUEC/LPSY/FTI) — Villahermosa's Preferred
- After the SOS breakout above Creek → wait for pullback to Creek.
- LPS/BUEC conditions: narrow ranges + low volume + price holds above Creek.
- This was Richard Wyckoff's PREFERRED entry because all structure is visible on the left.
- **Entry:** At appearance of SOS Bar on the confirmation pullback.
  Stop below LPS and below the broken Creek.
  Target: Phase E trend targets (based on cause measurement).

### Rule Z2.4: Zone 2 Stop and Target
- **LONG (LPS):** Stop below the LPS low AND below the broken Creek.
  Target = measured move based on accumulation structure width.
- **SHORT (LPSY):** Stop above the LPSY high AND above the broken Ice.
  Target = measured move based on distribution structure width.

### ZONE 3: PHASE E — Trend Out of Range (Highest Reliability, Lowest R:R)

### Rule Z3.1: Entry with SOS/SOW Bars in Trend
- After confirmation, during the Phase E trend movement.
- Enter on new SOS Bars (bullish) or SOW Bars (bearish) that appear during the trend.
- Highest reliability because you are trading in the direction of confirmed accumulation/distribution.

### Rule Z3.2: Entry with Minor Structures
- In Phase E uptrend: look for minor re-accumulation structures (lower timeframe).
- In Phase E downtrend: look for minor redistribution structures.
- These structures within the trend offer the safest entries.

### Rule Z3.3: Entry with Ordinary Shake
- Phase E shakes (Ordinary Shakeout / Ordinary Upthrust) serve the same function as
  Phase C shakes but with less preparation.
- **Actionable:** Same entry rules as Phase C but with smaller stop distances because

### Rule Z3.4: Zone 3 Stop and Target
- **LONG:** Stop below the minor structure low or the LPS low.
  Target = next major liquidity zone (previous high) or cause-based measurement.
- **SHORT:** Stop above the minor structure high or the LPSY high.

## SECTION 8 — CREEK AND BUEC RULES [VILLAHERMOSA — NEW]

### Rule CK1: Creek Definition
- The CREEK is the resistance level created by the Automatic Rally (AR) high.
- In accumulation, the Creek is the barrier the price must "jump across" to confirm
  the structure (hence "Jump Across the Creek" = JAC).
- **Actionable:** Draw the Creek at the AR high. All resistance tests within the range
  interact with this level.

### Rule CK2: Ice Definition
- The ICE is the support level created by the Automatic Reaction (AR) low.
- In distribution, the Ice is the floor the price must "fall through" to confirm breakdown.
- **Actionable:** Draw the Ice at the AR low. All support tests within the range
  interact with this level.

### Rule CK3: BUEC (Back Up to the Edge of the Creek)
- After the SOS/JAC breaks above the Creek, price pulls back to test it.
- The BUEC IS the LPS (Last Point of Support) — same event, different name.
- Ideal BUEC behavior: narrow ranges, low volume, price holds above Creek.
- **Entry rule:** Buy at the BUEC if volume on pullback < 75% of breakout volume
  AND price does not re-enter the range.

### Rule CK4: Failed Creek Break
- If price attempts to break Creek but faces strong opposition (high volume blocking):
  1. Price pulls back to LPS within range → "takes a running start" → tries again.
  2. The attempted break fails completely → becomes a new Shake in the opposite direction.
- **Actionable:** Failed Creek break + re-entry into range = structure NOT confirmed.
  Reassess — the accumulation may be failing.

## SECTION 9 — SHAKEOUT VS SPRING RULES [VILLAHERMOSA — NEW]

### Rule SS1: Spring vs Terminal Shakeout
| Feature | Spring #3 (Best) | Spring #2 | Terminal Shakeout (#1) |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|----------------------|

3. Check recovery: Immediate (1-2 bars) = Spring, Extended (3+ bars) = Shakeout.
- **Actionable:** For algorithmic detection:
  IF penetration < 3% AND volume < SC volume AND recovery within 3 bars → SPRING
  IF penetration > 3% OR volume > SC volume OR recovery > 3 bars → SHAKEOUT → WAIT FOR TEST

## SECTION 10 — RE-ACCUMULATION VS DISTRIBUTION [VILLAHERMOSA — NEW]

### Rule RD1: The Critical Distinction
After an uptrend pauses into a lateral range, is this:
- RE-ACCUMULATION (continuation pattern → uptrend resumes), OR
- DISTRIBUTION (reversal pattern → downtrend begins)?

### Rule RD2: Re-Accumulation Characteristics
- Appears AFTER an uptrend stops (not after a prolonged decline).
- Volume DECREASES progressively throughout the range (like accumulation).
- Higher lows tend to form within the range (buyers dominant).
- Duration depends on the ratio of strong hands vs weak hands.
- Phase C spring may be a minor event (doesn't need to sweep extreme).
- **Actionable:** If the range appears after an uptrend AND volume is decreasing
  AND higher lows forming → likely RE-ACCUMULATION = BULLISH continuation.

### Rule RD3: Distribution Characteristics
- Also appears after an uptrend — this is what makes it confusing.
- Volume remains HIGH and VOLATILE throughout the range.
- Lower highs tend to form within the range (sellers dominant).
- Upthrusts fail to reach previous peaks with conviction.
- **Actionable:** If the range appears after an uptrend AND volume is PERSISTENTLY HIGH
  AND lower highs forming → likely DISTRIBUTION = BEARISH reversal.

### Rule RD4: Key Distinguishing Factors
| Signal | Re-accumulation | Distribution |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Volume character | Decreasing progressively | Persistently high/volatile |
| Internal structure | Higher lows within range | Lower highs within range |
| Selling waves | Diminishing (less volume each time) | Increasing (more volume each time) |
| Buying waves | Steady or increasing | Diminishing |
| Background | Preceded by confirmed accumulation below | May not have clear accumulation below |
| Duration | Proportional to strong/weak hand ratio | Usually longer |
| Spring behavior | Works quickly, clean recovery | If it works, slow and messy recovery |

### Rule RD5: The Definitive Test
- If the breakout from the range is UPWARD with expanding volume → Re-accumulation confirmed.
- If the breakout is DOWNWARD with expanding volume → Distribution confirmed.
- **Actionable:** Until the breakout occurs, use the distinguishing factors above to
  BIAS your expectations, but do not commit fully until Phase D confirms direction.

## SECTION 11 — FAILED STRUCTURE RULES [VILLAHERMOSA — NEW]

### Rule FS1: Failed Accumulation
- The Spring/Shake test in Phase C shows HIGH volume → opposing side still strong.
- After the test, price FAILS to develop the expected SOS/JAC breakout.
- Instead, price breaks below the Spring low (new low).
- **Actionable:** If price breaks below the Spring low on expanding volume →
  ACCUMULATION HAS FAILED. The decline will continue. EXIT all longs.

### Rule FS2: Failed Distribution
- The UTAD test shows HIGH volume → buyers still strong.
- After the test, price FAILS to develop the expected SOW breakdown.
- Instead, price breaks above the UTAD high (new high).
- **Actionable:** If price breaks above the UTAD high on expanding volume →
  DISTRIBUTION HAS FAILED. The advance will continue. EXIT all shorts.

### Rule FS3: Failed Breakout (Phase D Failure)
- The SOS/SOW appears to break the Creek/Ice but immediately reverses.
- Price re-enters the range on the next few bars.
- **This failed break becomes a new Shaking event for the opposite direction.**
- **Actionable:** If SOS breaks Creek then price re-enters range with high volume →
  the "SOS" was actually a UTAD for a distributive structure developing inside.
  REVERSE your bias.

### Rule FS4: Secondary Test Failure
- When the ST after the Climax shows volume EQUAL TO or GREATER than the Climax:
  - Supply/demand is NOT exhausted.
  - Phase A is NOT complete.
  - Expect Phase B to begin, or the structure to fail entirely.
- **Actionable:** Volume on ST ≥ volume on SC/BC → DO NOT trust Phase A completion.
  Wait for additional tests with declining volume.

## SECTION 13 — POSITION MANAGEMENT RULES [VILLAHERMOSA]

### Rule PM1: Send All Three Orders Simultaneously
- Entry + Stop Loss + Take Profit → ALL submitted at once.
- Prevents emotional modification of stops after entry.
- Prevents electronic disconnection leaving unprotected position.

### Rule PM2: Position Sizing Based on Risk
- Calculate: Risk per trade = 1% of account SIZE.
- Distance from entry to stop loss = RISK DISTANCE.
- Position size = Risk amount ÷ Risk distance.
- Example: $5000 account, 1% risk = $50. If stop is $2 away, size = 25 shares.

### Rule PM3: Stop Loss Placement by Entry Type
| Entry Type | Stop Loss Location |
|------------|-------------------|
| Spring entry | Below the Spring low |
| Spring test entry | Below SOSbar OR below Spring low (choose wider for safety) |
| UTAD entry | Above the UTAD high |
| UTAD test entry | Above SOWbar OR above UTAD high |
| LPS/BUEC entry | Below SOSbar AND below the broken Creek |
| LPSY/FTI entry | Above SOWbar AND above the broken Ice |
| Minor structure entry | Below/above the entire minor structure extreme |

### Rule PM4: Take Profit Methods
1. **Climactic Evidence:** Extreme volume + wide range bar at trend extreme → EXIT.
2. **Phase A of New Structure:** First four events of a new range appearing → EXIT.
3. **Liquidity Zones:** Previous significant highs/lows (where orders cluster) → PARTIAL EXIT.
4. **Volume Profile Levels:** VPOC, VWAP, VAH/VAL of the structure → PARTIAL EXIT.
5. **Cause-based measurement:** P&F count from the structure width → FULL EXIT area.

### Rule PM5: Scaling Into Positions
- Zone 1 (Phase C): Small initial position (highest risk, highest R:R).
- Zone 2 (Phase D confirmed): Add to position (structure confirmed).
- Zone 3 (Phase E): Add on LPS/LPSY pullbacks within the trend.
- **Move stop to breakeven** on original position when Zone 2 entry triggers.

### Rule PM6: Trailing Stop Rules
- Villahermosa does NOT particularly recommend trailing stops (they get hit too easily).
- PREFERRED: Use Phase A events of a new structure as exit signal.
- If using trailing stops: place below each successive LPS (uptrend) or above each LPSY (downtrend).
