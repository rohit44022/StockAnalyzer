Mental Game of Trading

Based on Jared Tendler's book • "Am I trading my system — or my emotions?"

What is this system?
This is your Personal Trading Psychology Tracker. Most traders lose money not because of bad strategies, but because emotions (fear, greed, revenge, overconfidence) hijack their decision-making. This system helps you:
✓ Rate your mental state before each trading day (score 1-10)
✓ Check yourself before every trade entry (5-point gate)
✓ Track which emotions you felt during trades and whether you followed your rules
✓ Detect patterns automatically (e.g. "you've been revenge-trading 3 days in a row")
✓ Adjust your position size automatically based on your mental state

How is this connected to your Portfolio Tracker?
When you add a new trade in the Portfolio Tracker, you'll see optional psychology fields (your emotion + mental score). When you close a trade, you'll see fields for whether you followed your system, pattern tags, and a reflection. These get automatically saved here in the Mental Game database. So your portfolio and psychology stay linked — every trade has both financial AND emotional data.

What is a Pre-Session Checklist?
Before you even open a chart, you need to check: "Am I mentally fit to trade today?" Just like a pilot runs through a pre-flight checklist before takeoff, you run through these 10 questions before every trading session. They check things like: Did you sleep well? Are you under financial stress? Are you trying to recover yesterday’s losses? Are you feeling overconfident from a winning streak?

How does the verdict work?
• 8+ checked = GO — You’re in good mental shape. Trade normally at full size.
• 5-7 checked = REDUCED — Yellow flag. You can trade, but with smaller positions (50-75% size) and fewer trades.
• Less than 5 = NO-GO — Your head is not in the right place. Do NOT trade today. Study charts, review notes, but don’t risk real money.

Mental Score (1-10): After completing the checklist, rate your overall mental clarity on a scale of 1-10. This number directly controls how much of your portfolio you’re allowed to risk today. Score 10 = full size. Score 5 = half size, 1 trade only. Score 4 or below = no trading at all.

What is the Pre-Trade Psychological Gate?
The Pre-Session checklist was for the whole day. This gate is for each individual trade. Before you click "Buy" or "Sell," you must pass all 5 checks. Think of it as a bouncer at a nightclub — if you can't pass every check, you don't get in.

The 5 checks are: (1) My system shows a valid signal, (2) I have enough technical confluence (multiple indicators agree), (3) My mental score is high enough, (4) I’m not emotionally compromised, (5) This trade fits my risk management rules.

All 5 must be checked = PASS. Even 4 out of 5 = BLOCKED. No exceptions. This is the hardest rule to follow because your emotions will scream "just take the trade!" — but the gate exists precisely for those moments.

Log Trade Psychology: After each trade, log what you felt, whether you followed your system, and if you made any psychological mistakes. This data feeds the pattern detection system, which automatically warns you when you're repeating mistakes.

Portfolio Connection: When you add/close trades in the Portfolio Tracker, the psychology fields there (emotion, mental score, system followed, pattern tag, reflection) are automatically saved here too. You don't have to enter data twice.

What are In-Trade Psychological Rules?
You've entered a trade. Now what? This is where most traders lose control. Your stock starts dropping and panic sets in. Or it spikes up and greed tells you to add more. These rules tell you exactly what to do when specific emotions hit while you’re in a live trade.

Each rule card below shows a Situation (e.g., "Trade moves against you and you feel panic") and a Rule (e.g., "Check your stop-loss. If it's not hit, do nothing. The system handles exits, not your emotions."). These are your guardrails while the trade is live.

The 4-Step Real-Time Strategy is your emergency brake for when emotions spike mid-trade:
1. RECOGNIZE — Notice and name the emotion ("I am feeling revenge right now").
2. DISRUPT — Break the momentum: deep breaths, stand up, walk away from the screen for 60 seconds.
3. INJECT LOGIC — Say your pre-prepared correction statement out loud (not improvised — one you wrote in advance).
4. STRATEGIC REMINDER — List 3 mistakes you always make when this emotion takes over.

What is a Performance Map?
Think of this as your personal mistake encyclopedia. Every trader makes recurring mistakes — the same 3-5 errors over and over. The Performance Map forces you to write down each mistake, figure out why you make it (the real emotional root cause), and prepare a correction statement to use when you catch yourself doing it again.

The 5 fields explained:
• Mistake = What bad thing do you keep doing? (e.g., "I chase entries after seeing a big green candle")
• Emotion Behind It = What feeling drives this mistake? (e.g., "FOMO — fear of missing out on the move")
• Root Cause = The deep psychological reason (e.g., "I equate missing a trade with being a bad trader")
• Correction Statement = What you say to yourself when you catch it (e.g., "Missing a trade costs nothing. A bad entry costs capital AND confidence.")
• Early Warning = How do you know it's about to happen? (e.g., "When I open a chart after seeing a move on Twitter and feel urgency")
• Severity (1-10) = How much damage does this mistake cause when it happens? (10 = catastrophic, 1 = minor)

What is the Mental Score System?
Every trading day, you rate yourself from 1 to 10. This isn't random — each number has a specific meaning and directly controls what you're allowed to do. It’s like a traffic light for your trading:

• Score 8-10 (Green Light) — You’re clear-headed. Trade at full position size, normal rules apply.
• Score 5-7 (Yellow Light) — You’re not at your best. Reduce position size, require more technical evidence, and limit number of trades.
• Score 1-4 (Red Light) — You’re emotionally compromised. DO NOT TRADE. No position size, no entries. Study only.

Why? Research on performance psychology (Yerkes-Dodson Law) shows humans make optimal decisions only within a narrow emotional band. Too stressed or too euphoric = bad decisions. This system prevents you from trading outside that optimal window.

Each score below shows: what it means, what position size is allowed, and what action to take.

What are Emergency Protocols?
These are your "break glass in case of emergency" procedures. Trading has extreme moments — big losses, streak losses, euphoric wins — and in those moments, your brain is NOT capable of rational decision-making. These 6 protocols tell you exactly what to do in each crisis situation:

• Protocol A — After a Large Loss: You just lost big money. You’re angry, scared, or desperate. The protocol says: STOP. Walk away. Don’t trade for at least 1 hour. Review what happened only after emotions settle.
• Protocol B — After 3+ Consecutive Losses: You’re in a losing streak. Cut position size by 50%. Take a break. Do NOT try to "make it back."
• Protocol C — After a Large Win: You feel amazing and invincible. This is DANGEROUS. Overconfidence leads to oversized positions. Lock in the win. Reduce size on next trade.
• Protocol D — After 3+ Consecutive Wins: Winning streak. Euphoria setting in. The protocol prevents you from doubling down and giving back all your profits.
• Protocol E — About to Break a Rule: You KNOW you’re about to do something stupid. Use the 4-step strategy: Recognize, Disrupt, Inject Logic, Strategic Reminder.
• Protocol F — Already Broke a Rule: Damage is done. Log it, identify the root cause, update your Performance Map, and plan how to prevent it next time.

Log every protocol usage below. Tracking when you use these (and whether you followed them) helps you measure emotional discipline over time.

What is the Weekly Psychological Report?
Every weekend, take 15 minutes to review your week — not just profits and losses, but your mental performance. This has two parts:

Auto-Computed Summary (Left Top): The system automatically calculates your weekly stats from the trades you logged: total trades, what % followed rules, average mental score, most common emotion, most common rule break, and active pattern alerts. You don’t need to calculate anything — it’s done for you.

Manual Weekly Report (Left Bottom): This is YOUR reflection. What was your biggest recurring pattern this week? What’s your ONE specific improvement goal for next week? Are things getting better, worse, or staying the same?

Why do this weekly? Daily reviews are too granular — you can’t see trends. Monthly is too late — you’ve already repeated mistakes for weeks. Weekly is the sweet spot for catching patterns and making adjustments before they compound into bigger problems.

What is the Learning Curve Tracker?
This tracks your psychological growth over months. Jared Tendler uses the concept of an "Inchworm" — imagine your performance as a range, like a worm. The front end is your best trading day (A-game). The back end is your worst (C-game). You improve NOT by making your best days better, but by making your worst days less bad. The worm "inches" forward from the back.

The 4 Competence Levels:
UI Unconscious Incompetence = You don’t even know you have the problem. You’re making the mistake and don’t realize it. ("I’m fine, I don’t revenge trade." But you do.)
CI Conscious Incompetence = You KNOW you have the problem but can’t stop it yet. ("I know I’m revenge trading but I can’t help it.")
CC Conscious Competence = You can control it, but it takes effort and active attention. ("I caught myself about to revenge trade and stopped.")
UC Unconscious Competence = RESOLVED. You don’t even think about it anymore. The correct behavior is automatic.

Monthly Assessment: Once a month, rate yourself on 5 dimensions: Fear, Greed, Anger/Tilt, Confidence, and Discipline. For each one, select which competence level you’re currently at. Over time, you should see them move from UI→CI→CC→UC. That’s the inchworm moving forward.

What is the Daily Workflow?
This is your complete daily routine that combines both technical analysis AND psychological preparation in the right order. Think of it as your trading day recipe — follow these steps in order, every day, and you’ll build the habits that separate professional traders from amateurs.

Morning → Before Trade → During Trade → After Trade → End of Session → Weekly → Monthly

Each phase has specific steps. The key insight: psychology goes BEFORE technical analysis. You check your mental state first, then look at charts. If your mental check fails, you don’t even open the charts. This is backwards from what most traders do (charts first, then realize mid-trade they’re emotional), but it’s the correct order.

Morning = Pre-session checklist + mental score + warmup.
Before Trade = Pre-trade gate (5 checks) before every entry.
During Trade = Use in-trade rules + 4-step strategy if emotions spike.
After Trade = Log trade psychology (what you felt, system followed, pattern tag).
End of Session = Cooldown, review the day, update mental score.
Weekly = Write weekly report, review patterns and progress.
Monthly = Update learning curve, rate competence levels.

How Does Psychology Connect to Your Technical Trading System?
This is where the Mental Game system directly integrates with your Portfolio Tracker and technical analysis. Most psychology systems are separate from trading — just a journal that sits in a corner. This system is different: your mental score actually changes what you’re allowed to do in real-time.

Connection 1 — Confluence Scoring: Your technical analysis uses confluence (how many indicators agree on a trade). When your mental score is low, the system raises the bar — you need MORE technical evidence to justify a trade. Score 8+ = need 4/5 confluence. Score 7 = need 5/5 (perfect setup only). Score 1-4 = no trading.

Connection 2 — Position Sizing: Your mental score controls how much money you’re allowed to risk per trade. Feeling great (9-10)? Full position size. Feeling off (6-7)? Half to three-quarter size. Emotionally compromised (1-4)? Zero — no trades at all.

Connection 3 — Pattern Detection: Every trade you log with a pattern tag (FOMO, revenge trade, etc.) feeds the automatic pattern detection engine. If the same pattern appears 3+ times = 50% size reduction on all trades. 5+ times = STOP trading until pattern is resolved.

Connection 4 — Rule Break Feedback Loop: When you mark "System Followed = NO" on any trade, it triggers the full feedback cycle: tag the pattern → identify root cause → run Emergency Protocol F → update Performance Map → update Learning Curve. This turns every mistake into a concrete learning opportunity.
